03 — Design Approach

Technology Guide — Choosing the Right Display Medium

07 / 20

Selection of the right technology is a trade-off between ambient light performance, resolution, and budget. We assess the entire market to design a bespoke ecosystem for your space.

🔦

Projection

High-power projectors (up to 20,000 lumens) cast ultra-wide images at a fraction of the cost of LED per square metre — ideal for creating dramatic, large-format experiences on a defined budget.

Advantages

Massive scale at the most competitive budget. Best cost-per-square-metre across all solutions. Ultra-wide wrap and CAVE configurations achievable.

Considerations

Requires ambient light control (blinds/blackout) for full impact. Changes room dynamics from open office to dedicated theatre mode.

Recommended
💡

LED Panels

Direct-view LED panels deliver unparalleled brightness — visible and impactful even in fully lit office environments. The seamless, bezel-free surface creates a premium, gallery-quality aesthetic.

Advantages

Unparalleled brightness — fully usable in lit-office conditions. Seamless surface with no visible bezels. Premium aesthetic matching the quality of the development.

Considerations

Higher cost per square metre. Best value when the surface is used daily and ambient-light-control is not practical.

🖥️

LCD Video Wall

Tiled LCD panels provide the highest pixel density of any display medium — ideal for rendering detailed architectural drawings, fine-grain technical content, and floor plans with precision.

Advantages

Highest resolution for technical drawings. Excellent for detailed floor plan presentation. Competitive cost relative to LED for equivalent area.

Considerations

Visible bezels (grid lines) between screens break the immersive illusion for video content. Acceptable for data-heavy, technical presentations.

04 — Canvas Intelligence

08 / 20

Intelligent
Content
Management

The software layer automatically adapts to your screen configuration — whether single wall, corner, or full wrap. Content is routed intelligently so 3D models, presentations, and branded collateral are always displayed in the optimal zone without manual intervention.

Zero-configuration principle

No technician required on-site. The system treats any combination of screens as a single unified canvas, making context-appropriate decisions automatically.

01

3D Immersive Mode

Your masterplan fills the entire physical view — offering a "cinematic window" into the future development. On corner and wrap configurations, the system utilises the geometry to create a genuine parallax 3D depth effect.

02

Multiple Presentations (Picture-in-Picture)

One screen becomes the working surface for floor plans and specifications, while the remaining screens maintain the emotional connection with atmospheric CGI or live 3D views. Dual-presenter mode allows simultaneous cross-referencing.

03

2D Content Management

Standard PDFs, presentations, and videos snap to fit the screen height automatically. On multi-screen setups, documents are intelligently constrained to a single wing — text never stretches across a corner fold, ensuring perfect legibility.

04

Branded Collateral & Idle States

Idle states display full-screen, high-resolution brand loops or static holding slides — setting the tone before the meeting begins. Across multi-screen configurations, brand wallpaper wraps the viewer in your identity from the moment they enter.

Operation

Complexity should be invisible. The system is designed to minimise interaction points — the agent running the pitch should never have to act as a technician. A single, branded interface puts them in complete control.

Instant transition between 3D immersive mode and 2D presentation content. No menus, no configuration — one tap.

Layer technical data, floor plans, or leasing schedules over the live 3D model without interrupting the immersive experience.

Zero Setup

No technician required on-site. The system boots to a ready state and the agent can begin presenting immediately.

📱

Presenter App

Agent-held tablet or phone acts as the remote control, allowing natural movement around the room while maintaining full control of the presentation narrative.
